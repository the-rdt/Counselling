How our JEE Counselling Tools work

  • Choice filling tool

    This tool helps you to practice the filling of choices as done in JoSAA Counselling website. Upon clicking this icon, you will be forwarded to a window requesting you to choose from your preferred institutes, category, seat-pool and preferred branch.
    And after choosing accordingly, click on the submit option. You will be provided with the a table of selected Institutes, respective branches, and opening and closing cut-off of all 7 JoSAA rounds. You can sort the rows in according to the columns of JoSAA cut-off opening and closing ranks.
    By practicing beforehand, you will be able to get a clear picture of how counselling takes place and you will be able to fill your choices without any doubts or faults!

  • Rank predictor

    The Rank Predictor tool helps you to predict the most probable rank based on your percentile. You have to fill your percentile and your category in the dialog box appearing and click on submit option. You will be provided with an expected JEE Main rank based on the previous year ranks data.

    Note: This is just an anticipated rank based on previous year ranks and not an exact rank! Our Predictions Models are designed and trained by expert students with counselling experience. We made our prediction models better than other websites and fixed many traditional loopholes that they have.

  • College predictor

    This tool helps you to predict your preferred college based on your Percentile/Rank and Category. After clicking on the rank predictor icon, submit your percentile/rank and your category in the dialogue box appearing.
    On comparing your percentile and ranks with the top affecting factors like last year cut-offs of each JoSAA rounds, average placement of the institutes, highest placement of the institutes, increased no of seats every year and female quota increment you will be provided with few institutes and their respective presumed branches.

  • College comparator

    This tool provides you with the right comparison of IIIT’s. You have to select the two IIIT’s that you want to compare from the dialog box provided. And you will be getting the information about each IIIT based on their academic curriculum, performance, placements, college life, etc. Whereby, you can choose your appropriate college and branch while filling your choices.

  • Questions to IIITians

    You can post your queries to us by clicking on this icon. You have to fill few details of yours as, your full-name, email-ID, and your query. You have to select weather your question is related to any particular IIIT or a general question. The question will be forwarded to our team from that particular institute and you will get reply from them with email. The questions as well as their answers will be reflected in the website as well.

IIIT's at a glance

  • IIITs Funding

    IIITs at Gwalior, Allahabad, Jabalpur, Kurnool and Kancheepuram are fully government funded IIITs. Rest of the IIITs are based on the public-private partnership (PPP) model, funded by the central government, state governments and industry partners in the ratio 50:35:15.

  • What is PPP model?

    A public private partnership involves both government and private organizations working together to complete a project. New IIITs are funded by the central government, state governments and private organizations.

  • IIITs Vs Other Colleges.

    IIIITs, unlike other major government funded colleges, are a group of institutes that are focused mainly on information technology. They have a curriculum strictly aligned as per the needs of the IT industry.

  • Courses

    Most of the IIITs offer undergraduate courses in Computer Science & Engineering, Electronics & Communication Engineering and Information Technology. However some IIITs also offer undergraduate courses in design (IIIT-Jabalpur), mechanical engineering (IIIT-Kancheepuram), etc.

  • No Management Quota

    No, for undergraduate admission in any IIIT, one must give the JEE-Main exam and take part in the JOSAA counselling.

  • Curriculum

    The curriculum is strictly aligned to the needs of the IT industry. It is geared at keeping abreast with the latest developments in research and IT industry.

  • Campus

    Few IIITs have their own campus. Others are running in a temporary campus situated in some other colleges (like various IITs / NITs), until the construction of their campus is complete.
